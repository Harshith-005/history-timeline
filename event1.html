1903 — Wright Brothers' First Flight

December 17, 1903 — the first sustained powered flight.

Overview

The Wright brothers — Orville and Wilbur — flew the first controlled, heavier-than-air, powered aircraft at Kitty Hawk, North Carolina. Their work laid the foundation for modern aeronautics.

Key facts

  • Date: December 17, 1903
  • Location: Kitty Hawk, North Carolina
  • Significance: Beginning of powered flight and aviation industry.

Impact

This achievement accelerated developments in transportation, military technology, and global travel — reshaping 20th century society.
